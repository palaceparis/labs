Let XX be a random variable representing the number of boys in a family with 4 children. The chi-squared goodness-of-fit test to test if XX follows a binomial distribution is

  1. Hypothesis: H0:H0: XX follows a binomial distribution vs H1:H1: XX does not follow a binomial distribution.

  2. Assumptions: independent observations (independent rolls of the 4 dice) and ei=npi≥5ei=npi≥5.

  3. Test statistic: T=∑i=1k(Yi−ei)2eiT=∑i=1k(Yi−ei)2ei. Under H0H0, T∼χ2k−1T∼χk−12 approx.

  4. Observed test statistic: t0=13.16t0=13.16

  5. p-value: P(χ24≥13.16)=0.01052P(χ42≥13.16)=0.01052

  6. Decision: Since the p-value is less than 0.05, we reject the null hypothesis. The data is not consistent with the null hypothesis that the data follow a binomial distribution with probability of success, p=0.5p=0.5.

This is a sensible conclusion as the data were actually generated using:

set.seed(10)
y = table(rbinom(100,4,0.55))
